# Domestic Money Transfer — Standard Operating Procedure

## Overview
Eko enables retailers to offer instant domestic money transfers. Customers can send money from cash to any bank account in India using IMPS (Immediate Payment Service) or NEFT (National Electronic Fund Transfer).

## How to Process a Money Transfer

### Step-by-Step
1. Open the Eko Connect app
2. Go to "Money Transfer" section
3. Enter the recipient's bank account number
4. Select the recipient's bank (IFSC code will auto-fill)
5. Enter the transfer amount
6. Collect cash from the customer (transfer amount + service charge)
7. Confirm the transaction details
8. Enter your Eko PIN to authorize
9. Transaction will be processed and receipt generated
10. Print or share the receipt with the customer

### Transaction Limits
- Minimum transfer: ₹100
- Maximum per transaction: ₹5,000 (may vary based on retailer tier)
- Maximum per day per customer: ₹25,000
- Maximum per month per customer: ₹1,00,000

### Transfer Modes
- **IMPS**: Instant (24/7, including holidays). Amount credited within seconds.
- **NEFT**: Bank working hours only. Settlement in batches (every 30 minutes).

## Common Money Transfer Issues

### Transfer Failed — Money Not Sent
**Cause**: Bank server rejection or incorrect account details.
**Solution**:
1. Verify the account number and IFSC code
2. Check if the recipient's bank account is active
3. Try again — bank servers may have temporary issues
4. Your wallet will not be debited for failed transactions

### Transfer Successful But Recipient Didn't Receive
**Cause**: IMPS delay or wrong account details.
**Solution**:
1. Check the transaction receipt — note the UTR/reference number
2. IMPS: Usually instant, but can take up to 30 minutes in some cases
3. NEFT: Settlement happens in batches, may take 2–4 hours
4. If not received after 4 hours, ask the recipient to check with their bank using the UTR number
5. If still unresolved after 24 hours, contact cs@eko.co.in with the transaction ID

### Wrong Account Number
**Cause**: Retailer entered incorrect account number.
**Solution**:
1. If the transaction is already processed, you cannot reverse it directly
2. Contact cs@eko.co.in immediately with:
   - Transaction ID
   - Correct vs incorrect account number
   - Amount and date
3. Eko will initiate a reversal request with the bank
4. Note: Reversals depend on the receiving bank's cooperation and typically take 7–14 working days

### Daily Limit Exceeded
**Cause**: Customer has exceeded their daily or monthly transfer limit.
**Solution**:
1. Check the current limits in the Eko Connect app
2. The customer can try again the next day
3. KYC-verified customers may have higher limits
4. Contact support if you need a limit increase for your retailer account

## Service Charges
- Charges are deducted from the retailer's Eko wallet
- The retailer collects the service charge from the customer
- Commission earned = Service charge collected - Eko's transaction fee
- Current rates are displayed in the Eko Connect app before each transaction

## Important Reminders
- Always verify account details with the customer before processing
- Never share your Eko PIN with anyone
- Keep transaction receipts for at least 30 days
- Report any suspicious transactions immediately to cs@eko.co.in
