# Eko CSP, KYC, and Wallet Block SOP

## CSP / Retailer Operations

Eko retailers act as local CSP-style service points for customers who need assisted financial services. A retailer should always confirm the service type before collecting cash: AePS cash withdrawal, balance enquiry, mini statement, DMT, bill payment, recharge, or account support.

For every assisted transaction:
1. Confirm the customer's mobile number and amount before proceeding.
2. Show the customer the transaction status on Eko Connect before handing over cash or closing the visit.
3. Share the transaction ID or receipt when available.
4. Never complete a second transaction only because the first screen is slow. First check Transaction Inquiry.
5. For disputes, collect transaction ID, date/time, customer mobile number, bank name, amount, and screenshot.

## Retailer KYC Pending or Rejected

If retailer KYC is pending:
1. Ask the retailer to open Profile > KYC Status in Eko Connect.
2. Verify PAN, Aadhaar, shop address, bank account name, and uploaded document clarity.
3. If any document is blurred, expired, mismatched, or cropped, upload a corrected document.
4. KYC re-verification generally takes 24 to 48 working hours after valid documents are submitted.
5. Do not advise the retailer to create a second account. Duplicate accounts can delay approval.

Escalate to Account Management if KYC has stayed pending for more than 3 working days after all documents are correct.

## Wallet Block or Debit Freeze

Wallet block, debit freeze, or transaction hold can happen because of incorrect KYC, suspicious activity, chargeback risk, failed settlement checks, or compliance review.

Immediate steps:
1. Tell the retailer not to retry high-value transactions until the block reason is confirmed.
2. Check whether the app shows "wallet blocked", "debit disabled", "compliance hold", or "KYC required".
3. Collect retailer ID, registered mobile number, wallet balance, last successful transaction, and screenshot.
4. If the block is due to KYC, guide document correction first.
5. If money is stuck or the retailer cannot serve customers, escalate as high priority.

Critical escalation is required if the retailer reports unauthorized wallet use, suspected fraud, SIM swap, stolen device, or money missing from wallet balance.

Assigned team:
- KYC pending/rejected: Account Management
- Wallet block due to compliance or KYC: Account Management
- Unauthorized wallet use or fraud: Security & Fraud
