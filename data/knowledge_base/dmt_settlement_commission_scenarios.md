# Eko DMT, Settlement, and Commission Scenarios

## DMT Money Sent but Beneficiary Did Not Receive

For Domestic Money Transfer (DMT), always check Transaction Inquiry before retrying or refunding cash.

Steps for the retailer:
1. Open Eko Connect > Transactions > Transaction Inquiry.
2. Search using transaction ID, customer mobile number, or date and amount.
3. If status is Success, ask the customer to check the beneficiary bank statement and share the UTR/reference number.
4. If status is Failed, wait for reversal to the retailer wallet or source as per the displayed timeline.
5. If status is Pending for more than 30 minutes, collect the transaction ID, sender mobile number, beneficiary bank, amount, and screenshot.

Escalate DMT pending cases as high priority when the amount is large, the customer is waiting at the shop, or the same retailer reports repeated pending transfers.

## Wrong Beneficiary Account

If the retailer sent money to a wrong beneficiary account:
1. Do not promise reversal.
2. Collect transaction ID, amount, sender mobile, beneficiary name/account/IFSC entered, and the correct beneficiary details.
3. Explain that successful IMPS/NEFT transfers depend on bank-side reversal or beneficiary consent.
4. Escalate to Technical Support/Bank Operations for tracing only when transaction status is Success with UTR.

## Settlement Delay

Settlement delay can affect retailer liquidity and should be treated carefully.

Checklist:
1. Confirm the settlement date, expected amount, and bank account mapped to the retailer.
2. Check whether the delay is for AePS, DMT commission, wallet transfer, or reversal.
3. Ask the retailer to verify bank holiday, account freeze, incorrect IFSC, or bank account name mismatch.
4. If settlement has crossed the promised T+1/T+2 working-day timeline, escalate to Finance Operations.
5. For repeated settlement failures, collect last three transaction IDs and bank statement screenshot.

## Commission Missing or Incorrect

Commission disputes require product-wise checks:
1. Confirm product: AePS, DMT, recharge, bill payment, insurance, or onboarding incentive.
2. Confirm transaction date, slab, amount, and retailer category.
3. Check if commission is paid instantly, daily, weekly, or after settlement cycle.
4. If commission is less than expected, compare against the active commission plan and GST/TDS deductions.
5. If commission is not visible after the cycle closes, escalate to Finance Operations with transaction IDs.

Escalation priority:
- Low: General commission rate question.
- Medium: Single transaction commission mismatch.
- High: Multiple missing commissions, settlement crossed SLA, or retailer cannot maintain wallet liquidity.
- Critical: Fraud/security concern, unauthorized transaction, or wallet balance missing.
