# Commission & Settlement — Guide

## How Commissions Work

### Commission Structure
As an Eko retailer, you earn commission on every successful transaction. Commission rates vary by service type:

- **AePS Cash Withdrawal**: ₹3–5 per transaction depending on amount
- **Money Transfer**: 0.3%–0.5% of transfer amount (minimum ₹5)
- **Bill Payments**: ₹2–10 per payment (varies by biller)
- **Mobile Recharge**: 2%–4% of recharge amount
- **DTH Recharge**: 2%–3% of recharge amount

Note: Commission rates are subject to periodic revision and are displayed in the Eko Connect app.

### Commission Calculation Example
If you process a money transfer of ₹5,000:
- Service charge collected from customer: ₹25
- Eko's processing fee: ₹10
- Your commission: ₹25 - ₹10 = ₹15

## Settlement Process

### What is Settlement?
Settlement is the process where your earned commissions are credited to your bank account. Eko settles commissions on a regular cycle.

### Settlement Cycle
- **Standard settlement**: Every Tuesday and Friday
- **Premium retailers**: Daily settlement (for retailers with high transaction volumes)
- **Minimum settlement amount**: ₹100
- **Settlement time**: Amounts are typically credited by 11 PM on settlement day

### How to Check Settlement Status
1. Open Eko Connect app
2. Go to "Commission" or "My Earnings" section
3. View pending vs settled commissions
4. Download detailed settlement reports for any date range

## Common Commission Issues

### Commission Not Credited
**Causes**:
- Settlement not yet due (check the settlement cycle)
- Bank account details incorrect in your profile
- Below minimum settlement threshold (₹100)
- Commission disputed or under review

**Solution**:
1. Verify your settlement cycle (Tuesday/Friday or daily)
2. Check if the settlement amount was above ₹100
3. Verify your bank account details in Settings > Bank Account
4. Wait until the next settlement date
5. If still not credited after the expected date, email cs@eko.co.in with:
   - Your Eko code
   - Expected settlement amount
   - Date range
   - Your bank account details (last 4 digits)

### Commission Amount Seems Wrong
**Causes**:
- Rate changes not reflected in your understanding
- Service charges deducted before commission
- Failed transactions don't generate commission
- TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) applied

**Solution**:
1. Check current commission rates in the Eko Connect app
2. Download your detailed commission report
3. Compare with your transaction history
4. Note: TDS at 5% is deducted on commission amounts exceeding ₹15,000 per year
5. If discrepancy persists, raise a ticket at cs@eko.co.in with the specific transactions in question

### Double Commission Deduction
If you notice a double deduction in your commission:
1. Note the specific transaction IDs involved
2. Check if there was a corresponding double transaction
3. Download your commission statement for the affected period
4. Email cs@eko.co.in with the specific dates and transaction IDs
5. The finance team will audit and reverse any incorrect deductions within 7 working days

## Wallet Management

### Adding Funds to Your Wallet
1. Go to "Add Money" in Eko Connect
2. Choose payment method (UPI, bank transfer, or debit card)
3. Enter the amount to add
4. Complete the payment
5. Wallet will be credited instantly (UPI) or within 30 minutes (bank transfer)

### Wallet Balance Issues
- Your wallet must have sufficient balance to process transactions
- Transaction amount + service charge is deducted from your wallet
- Commission is added back to your wallet after the settlement cycle
- You can view your wallet statement anytime in the app

## Tax Information
- Eko deducts TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) as per Indian tax laws
- TDS is deducted at 5% on commission exceeding ₹15,000 per financial year
- You can download your TDS certificate from the app at the end of the financial year
- File your income tax returns including Eko commission income
